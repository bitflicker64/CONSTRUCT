Welcome to NASA Images Portal

This Portal site offers a brief introduction to the type of data NASA makes available to the public.

APOD or Astronomy Picture of the Day is one of NASA’s most popular websites, providing daily images with explanations by astronomers.

The EPIC section provides information on daily imagery collected by DSCOVR’s Earth Polychromatic Imaging Camera. Learn more at EPIC.

The Library section gives access to NASA’s Image and Video Library. You can browse their extensive archives.

Astronomy Picture of the Day

One of the most popular websites at NASA is the Astronomy Picture of the Day. Each day a different image or photograph of our fascinating universe is featured, along with a brief explanation written by a professional astronomer. Click here

Asteroid Didymos and Dimorphos (NASA DART Mission)

NASA’s Double Asteroid Redirection Test (DART) captured this image of the binary asteroid system Didymos and its moon Dimorphos before impact.

EPIC

EPIC provides daily full-disc imagery of the Earth from the Earth–Sun Lagrange point.

This EPIC image shows a full natural-color view of Earth as seen from NASA’s DSCOVR spacecraft.
